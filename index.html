Electronic Health Records (EHR) play a crucial role in modern healthcare systems, providing healthcare providers with patient data to deliver effective care. However, the security and privacy of EHRs are paramount, as unauthorized access to sensitive patient information can have serious consequences. This paper presents a novel approach to enhance the security of EHRs by leveraging both Fog and Cloud servers.

The proposed system combines the advantages of Fog computing, which extends computing resources closer to the data source, and Cloud computing, which provides scalable and centralized resources. Fog servers are strategically placed in proximity to healthcare facilities and medical devices, allowing for real-time data processing and reducing latency. Cloud servers, on the other hand, offer high storage capacity and computational power for more intensive tasks and long-term data storage.
